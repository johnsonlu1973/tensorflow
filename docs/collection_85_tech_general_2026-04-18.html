Great white sharks are overheating
大白鯊正在過熱
The evolutionary edge that fueled great white shark dominance for millions of years could soon become its greatest downfall. The ocean’s most iconic predators maintain warmer body temperatures than the surrounding seawater and are paying an increasingly steep price for it. As the oceans warm due to climate change, they now face the risk of potentially fatal overheating, according to a new report in Science. Several large tuna species and sharks, known as “mesothermic” species for the way their bodies run hot, require more fuel to maintain their temperature and are thus confronting a “double jeopardy” of warming oceans and declining food, mainly from overfishing. As water temperatures climb, these species will be forced to relocate to cooler waters. Read full article Comments
曾經推動大白鯊統治海洋數百萬年的演化優勢可能很快成為其最大弱點，這些海洋頂級掠食者維持比周圍海水更高的體溫，隨著海洋暖化代價愈來愈高。
